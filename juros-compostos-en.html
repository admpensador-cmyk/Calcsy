How does this calculator work?

It projects the future value of an investment using compound interest. You specify how much you will invest initially, whether you will make monthly contributions, what the monthly rate is and for how many months the money will remain invested.

The formula used is the compound capitalization formula, adding the growth of the initial amount and contributions month by month.

When you click calculate the tool not only shows the final values but also generates a chart of the month‑by‑month evolution. That way you clearly see how the investment grows over time and how the accumulated interest accelerates after a few periods.

What do the fields mean?

  • Initial amount: how much you already have available to invest.
  • Monthly contribution: how much you intend to add every month (can be 0).
  • Monthly rate (%): the estimated monthly return.
  • Months: the duration of the investment.

How to read the result

Future value is the total at the end of the period. Total contributed shows how much of the total came from your pocket. Interest earned is how much the money “worked” for you.
